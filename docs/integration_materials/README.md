see notes: docs\integration_materials\cooperative-projects

see template: https://github.com/WebCivics/CooperativeProjects  however note, that this template is based upon a paradigm where its used for a company, not a decentralised platform where each individual has their own structures, contributes to projects they find in their own way, defining some projects, contributing in some way to others, etc.  So, the implementation needs to remap the logic, language, flows, etc.  to reflect this significant change. 

Also, the personsal book-keeping / accounting systems, should be able to help consider the cost of resources, etc. as may be employed for different projects, or made available for projects; for example, AI platforms have a certain amount of tokens, which get consumed for particular tasks; or,the cost of a laptop over a period of time, or the cost of a starlink connection, etc. etc. etc. 

The cooperative projects work, should provide the fundamental needs for people to find and start projects they're interested in pursuing, and the associated structures to get them going.  This is particularly also focused on 'humanitarian ict' projects, and related 'web civics' activities, etc.  

Where, the objective is that people are fairly compensated (obligation costs) but not renumerated in perpetuity. 

Licenses may use micropayments, may apply costs to particular types of users (ie: corporate users, companies, legal personalities - but not human beings, natural legal persons) using particular patterns, that might use the wellfair app to evaluate circumstances, without disclosing sensitive information.
